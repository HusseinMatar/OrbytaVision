Explore the wonders of the universe

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the heart of our solar system. Its gravity holds the solar system together and it provides energy for all life on Earth.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun, and the smallest planet in our solar system. It's only slightly larger than Earth's Moon. It's the star at the center of our solar system, providing energy for all life on Earth.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, and our closest planetary neighbor. It's the hottest planet in our solar system, and is sometimes called Earth's twin. It has a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat.

Earth

Earth, Our home planet – is the third planet from the Sun, and the fifth largest planet. It's the only place we know of inhabited by living things. It is mostly covered by water.

Mars

Mars – the fourth planet from the Sun – is a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere. This dynamic planet has seasons, polar ice caps, extinct volcanoes, canyons and weather. The Red Planet is known for its dusty landscapes and potential for life.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun, and the largest in the solar system – more than twice as massive as the other planets combined. It's famous for its Great Red Spot.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest planet in our solar system.It is best known for its stunning rings made of ice and rock.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, and the third largest planet in our solar system. It appears to spin sideways. It's a tilted ice giant that orbits the Sun on its side.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth, and most distant planet from the Sun. It’s the fourth-largest, and the first planet discovered with math. It's known for its deep blue color and strong winds.
